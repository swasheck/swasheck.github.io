Covid-19: Week 2

Mar 29, 2020 Categories: personal Tags: covid-19

Week 2 of quarantine and physical/“social” distancing is in the books (as of a few days ago). It's been an emotional roller coaster. So many of my other fears are still relevant, but they're reduced. This week my daughter has been battling the flu, though we couldn't really tell what was happening because symptoms for Covid-19 and just about every other thing seem so similar.

This week was a week of no sleep. My daughter would come into our room in the middle of the night and ask for help with things. Usually managing a burning, dry cough. We had a few chats with doctors and they didn't seem to think it was important enough to bring her anywhere for testing - the risk of exposure was far too high considering the symptoms she exhibited. She's feeling better now, but still coughing like a lifelong smoker. It'll be interesting to see if we can get her antibody-tested when/if this blows over.

I'm a Christian. Much of my frustration this week has been directed at other self-proclaimed Christians who are now prioritizing the specter of economic collapse over against catastrophic loss of humanity. The people of life seemed to be saying that death is a necessary sacrifice for the good of the economy. Additionally, the topic of “the end of the world” has come up in many of my Christian conversations. It's a weird conversation to have. Maybe it is. Maybe it isn't. Does the world really actually end, or is it actually remade - a la the New Perspective?

This has made for some sleepless nights and long conversations.

I do hope life returns to normal. I'm trying not to waste this time. I'm trying to self-learn NLP for Ancient and Koine Greek text analysis (I'm weird). I also have a pretty significant backlog of books to be reading.
